Privacy Policy

TabletopScore for iOS · Effective 20 July 2026

The short version. TabletopScore has no accounts and asks for nothing about you. It does not know your name, email, contacts, or location. The only thing it sends is an optional, anonymous count of which games and playlists get played — and you can switch that off in Settings. Nothing is sold or shared with anyone.

Who is responsible

TabletopScore is developed and operated by Oz Soffy, an independent developer. For any privacy question or request, contact ozsoffy@gmail.com.

What the app collects

1. Anonymous usage data (optional, can be switched off)

When the “Share anonymous usage data” setting is on, the app sends a small event each time you open a game or start playing a soundtrack. Each event contains only:

FieldWhat it is
typeWhich action happened: game_opened, play_started, or playlist_completed
gameId / playlistId / trackIdWhich catalog item it refers to (e.g. gloomhaven)
anonDeviceIdA random identifier created by the app on first launch
tsThe time the action happened

The anonDeviceId is a randomly generated value. It is not your Apple advertising identifier, and it is not derived from your device’s hardware or any account. Its only purpose is so that one person playing a playlist ten times isn’t counted as ten different people. Deleting the app deletes it; reinstalling generates a brand-new one, which cannot be connected to the previous one.

This data is used solely to understand which soundtracks are worth expanding. It is never used to build a profile, target advertising, or track you across other apps or websites.

2. Technical data from ordinary server requests

TabletopScore streams its music and artwork from its own server rather than bundling them in the app. As with any internet request, that server’s logs record the IP address, timestamp, and requested file for each request.

Please note: this happens whenever the app loads content, and is therefore not affected by the “Share anonymous usage data” setting — turning that setting off stops the usage events described above, but the app still has to contact the server to fetch music and images. These logs are used only to operate and secure the service, are not combined with the usage events above, and are not used to identify anyone.

What the app does not collect

  • No name, email address, phone number, or postal address
  • No account or sign-in of any kind — there is no login
  • No location data
  • No contacts, photos, calendar, microphone, or camera access
  • No advertising identifier (IDFA), and no App Tracking Transparency prompt, because the app does not track you
  • No health, financial, or biometric data
  • No third-party analytics, advertising, or social SDKs — the app uses only Apple’s own frameworks

How to turn analytics off

Open Settings → Privacy in the app and switch off “Share anonymous usage data.” This takes effect immediately: no further events are sent, and any events waiting to be sent are discarded.

Who the data is shared with

No one. The data is not sold, rented, traded, or shared with advertisers, data brokers, analytics vendors, or any other third party. It is stored on a server operated solely for TabletopScore, hosted on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, which acts only as the hosting provider.

How long it is kept

  • Usage events: retained in aggregate for product decisions. Because they contain no identifying information, they are not linked to you.
  • Server logs: kept only as long as needed to operate and secure the service, then rotated and deleted.
  • On your device: your settings and the anonymous identifier live in the app’s own storage and are removed completely when you delete the app.

Children

TabletopScore is suitable for general audiences and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because the app collects no personal information from anyone and has no accounts, messaging, or user-generated content, there is nothing about a child that could be collected or disclosed.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under the GDPR in the EU/UK, or the CCPA in California), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or export personal data held about you, and to object to its processing.

In practice, the app holds nothing that identifies you, so there is normally nothing to retrieve or erase. The most direct way to exercise these rights is to switch off analytics in Settings, and to delete the app — which removes the anonymous identifier permanently. If you would like to make a formal request or simply ask what is held, email ozsoffy@gmail.com and it will be answered.

Music

Music in TabletopScore is licensed under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0, with full credits listed in Settings → Music Credits. Playing music does not send anything to the composers or any third party — all audio is served from TabletopScore’s own server.

Changes to this policy

If what the app collects changes — for example if advertising is introduced in a future version — this policy will be updated before that version is released, and the effective date above will change. Material changes will also be noted in the app’s release notes.
